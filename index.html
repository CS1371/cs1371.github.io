What We Do

We are a team of CS 1371 TAs that develop software for the rest of the TA group. As a group, we are charged with creating and maintaining all internal software, which includes:

  • The Autograder
  • The Regrade Portal
  • Get Most Recent Files
  • The General TA Index

We are passionate about creating quality software, and will do our best to deliver on time, and exceed expectations. We are, however, also a group of learners. We will never know everything, but through diligence in our work and sincere enthusiasm to learn, we will better ourselves every day.

Why We Do It

Software is what separates a good group of CS TAs from a great group of TAs. Without computerized aid, a group of TAs, no matter how large or how competent, can only move so fast. A lack of software help limits what we can teach. As such, we are driven by a sense of purpose, which can be summed up as follows:

Make Teaching Simple

This drives everything we do. Each project, each documentation, and each line of code is an attempt to get closer to realizing this. We know that software can make a great TA group unstoppable, and so we shall strive to deliver helpful, quality software that makes a TA's life simple, intuitive, and maybe even fun! After all, nobody said developing software had to be boring

Additionally, we're also driven to learn more everyday. This team serves as a preparation for the "real world." Indeed, it seems that few, if any, universities that teach Computer Science also teach Software Development. Ironically, being able to develop better software together is something most employers expect of new hires on Day One. As such, it is our goal to "do things by the book" - in particular:

  • We will use best practices when coding. We'll have sensible conventions for naming of objects
  • We will use a Version Control System. In our case, we shall use git in conjunction with GitHub
  • We will follow industry standards when it comes to security. We will learn and utilize security measures such as GPG signing keys
  • We will learn about unit testing, and we will make Unit Testing an integral part of our workflow
  • We will write concise, thorough, and meaningful documentation for all of our code
  • We will write detailed specifications throughout the development process, and we will keep these specifications up to date
  • We will engage in cordial debate, and will not be afraid to question a decision

In an effort to accomplish these goals, we will use the following software. All software is either provided for free or through the Institute.

  • Trello - A Team Management platform that allows everyone to instantly see our progress in any project. You can see our Trello Team here
  • - A git-based remote platform that facilitates working together on different projects. Our GitHub Organization can be found here
  • MATLAB - The core of CS 1371, MATLAB will be our bread and butter. Wherever possbile, our code will be written in MATLAB
  • GPG (GnuPG) - A free software that facilitates the creation and validation of encryption keys for signing our work
  • Slack - Our main communications framework, Slack will allow easy and free communication among the entire group. You can access our group here
  • Google Drive - A free file hosting and editing platform, Google Drive allows simple methods for sharing and collaborating on common documents. To see our shared Drive, click here
